The G20 (Group of Twenty) is the premier international forum for global economic cooperation, representing around 85% of global GDP, 75% of international trade, and 80% of the world's population. Comprising 19 individual countries, the European Union, and the African Union, it focuses on policy coordination regarding trade, sustainable development, health, and climate. 2026 Summit: The 21st G20 Summit is scheduled to be held in Miami, USA, on December 14–15, 2026. Members: Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Republic of Korea, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, Türkiye, United Kingdom, United States, African Union, and the European Union. Purpose: Founded in 1999 to discuss global financial stability, it now addresses wider issues including, but not limited to, financial regulation, climate change, and sustainable development. Presidency: The presidency rotates annually among members, facilitating a "troika" (past, present, and future hosts) to ensure continuity.
