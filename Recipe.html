Brownies are rich, fudgy chocolate squares baked to perfection with a slightly crisp top and a soft, gooey center that melts in your mouth. Paired best with warm milk or ice cream, these treats are pure chocolate comfort in every bite. ğŸ«ğŸ’•

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 200g dark chocolate (chopped)
  • 3/4 cup (150g) sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Optional: walnuts or chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Melt butter and chocolate together over low heat until smooth.
  2. Remove from heat and whisk in sugar, then let cool slightly.
  3. Add eggs and vanilla, whisking until glossy.
  4. Fold in flour, cocoa powder, and salt (do not overmix).
  5. Pour into a lined baking tray.
  6. Bake at 180Â°C (350Â°F) for 20â€“25 minutes.
  7. Allow to cool before cutting into squares.

Tools Needed

  • Mixing bowl
  • Whisk or spatula
  • Baking tray
  • Parchment paper
  • Measuring cups/spoons

Tips

Do Not Overmix
Overmixing creates cakey brownies instead of fudgy ones.
Crackle Top
Whisking the sugar into warm chocolate helps create the shiny top.
Cooling Time
Allow brownies to cool completely for clean slices.
Add-ins
Nuts and chocolate chips add flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (100g) sugar
  • 1/2 cup (100g) brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Cream butter, sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  2. Mix in the egg and vanilla extract.
  3. Fold in flour, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Stir in chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop dough onto a lined baking tray.
  6. Bake at 180Â°C (350Â°F) for 10â€“12 minutes.
  7. Cool on tray before serving.

  • Baking tray
  • Parchment paper
  • Cooling rack

Chilling the dough helps cookies stay thick and chewy.

Room Temp Butter
Helps create the perfect texture.

Biscoff Cheesecake ğŸ°

Ingredients

  • 200g Biscoff biscuits (crushed)
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 400g cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whipping cream
  • 1/2 cup Biscoff spread (melted)

Steps

  1. Mix crushed biscuits with melted butter and press into a pan.
  2. Chill the crust in the fridge for 10 minutes.
  3. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Whip cream to soft peaks and fold into the cheese mixture.
  5. Pour filling over crust and smooth the top.
  6. Spread melted Biscoff over the top.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours or overnight.

Tools Needed

  • Springform pan
  • Hand mixer
  • Spatula
  • Mixing bowls

Tips

Cold Cream
Whipping cream must be cold for proper volume.
No-Bake
This cheesecake sets in the fridge â€” no baking needed.
Layer Smoothly
Warm Biscoff spread makes the topping easier to pour.

Ingredients

  • 200g ladyfinger biscuits
  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee (cooled)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Optional: coffee liqueur

Steps

  1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy.
  2. Fold in mascarpone and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Dip ladyfingers briefly in coffee.
  4. Layer soaked biscuits and cream in a dish.
  5. Repeat layers and finish with cream on top.
  6. Dust with cocoa powder.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving.

Tools Needed

  • Mixing bowl
  • Whisk
  • Baking or serving dish
  • Fine sieve (for cocoa dusting)

Tips

Do Not Oversoak

Chill Overnight
Creates better flavor and firmness.
Alcohol Option
A splash of coffee liqueur enhances flavor (optional).
